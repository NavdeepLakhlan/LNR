Our research

Breaking new ground in insect neurobiology & welfare

We are a team of neuroanatomists and physiologists using an interdisciplinary lens to address big questions in insect biology.

Themes in our work:

FATIGUE

We investigate the fatigue behavior of metallic materials under cyclic loading conditions. Our research focuses on understanding crack initiation and propagation mechanisms, especially in additively manufactured alloys, to enhance life prediction and structural integrity.

FRACTURE

Fracture mechanics plays a key role in evaluating material performance. We study how defects, microstructure, and loading conditions influence crack growth and catastrophic failure, aiming to develop materials with improved toughness and damage tolerance.

CREEP

Our work explores time-dependent deformation and failure under sustained stress and high temperature. We analyze creep mechanisms in engineering alloys to support long-term reliability in applications like aerospace, power plants, and structural components.
