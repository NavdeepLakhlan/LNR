Meet LNR

A Glimpse into the Mind and Journey of Prof. R. Lakshmi Narayan

Prof. R. Lakshmi Narayan

Hello! I'm Lakshmi — a curious mind with a passion for materials science, and a firm believer in learning by doing. My journey has taken me across academic institutions, industry collaborations, and international symposia, but at the heart of it all lies one goal: to ignite curiosity and critical thinking in young minds.

I currently serve as a faculty member at the Department of Materials Science and Engineering, IIT Delhi. I work at the exciting intersection of metal additive manufacturing, microstructure–property relationships, and advanced characterization.

“To me, science is not about answers—it’s about asking the right questions, and daring to explore the unknown with integrity and imagination.”

My Teaching Philosophy

Teaching, to me, is more than delivering lectures—it's about mentorship, dialogue, and mutual growth. I strive to create a space where questions are welcomed, mistakes are seen as learning milestones, and students are encouraged to challenge even the fundamentals.

Whether it’s in the lab or the classroom, I believe in learning by doing. I design courses and projects that bring theory to life—giving students the opportunity to build, break, analyze, and discover.

What Drives Me

Every alloy has a story, every micrograph a message. I find joy in interpreting those stories—be it through fracture surfaces, diffusion gradients, or phase maps. My research is driven by a desire to understand how structure governs behavior, and how we can use that understanding to engineer better materials.

I also deeply enjoy collaborative research—my work spans multiple countries, disciplines, and industries. I’ve been privileged to guide young researchers who have gone on to make significant contributions of their own.

Beyond the Lab

When I’m not peering into a microscope or scribbling in my research notebook, you’ll find me enjoying Carnatic music, taking long nature walks, or catching up on philosophy and history books.

Ultimately, I believe science is a human endeavor—rooted in humility, shaped by community, and propelled by wonder.
